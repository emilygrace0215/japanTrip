Friday
Tokyo
Akihabara + Ochanomizu Guitar Street
😴
Sleep in, late breakfast Jet lag is real, no point fighting it. Slow morning with a big konbini breakfast or a kissaten (old-school coffee shop) before heading out.
🎮
Akihabara — afternoon wander Wild energy unlike anywhere else. Specific stops: Mandarake (7 floors, vintage + current anime goods, great for Eva and Chainsaw Man), AmiAmi Figure Tower (opened 2024, 8 floors of figures, huge Eva and CSM selection), Animate Akihabara (biggest Animate branch, good for current merch), GiGO arcade (crane games with Chainsaw Man prizes). Cat merch is everywhere; if we want an actual cat cafe, Nekobukuro in Ikebukuro is worth a detour another day.
⛩️
Kanda Myojin Shrine 5 min walk from Akihabara. A beautiful shrine that literally blesses your electronics. Quirky and uncrowded.
🎸
Ochanomizu Guitar Street One stop from Akihabara on the Chuo line — easily combined. About 50 instrument shops packed onto one street, the biggest guitar district in Japan. Key stops: Ishibashi Music (flagship store, wall-to-wall Gibson and Fender), Shimokura (650 guitars on display at all times, great used floor), Kurosawa (the main Martin dealer in Japan — some rosewood models only sold here and can't leave the country). Staff encourage playing everything. Victor will need a time budget here.
🌇
Evening: easy neighborhood night Early night is fine given the travel from Da Nang. Ameyoko for dinner or just konbini and decompress. Save the energy for tomorrow.

Tokyo Dome
Baseball Day ⚾
🌇
Morning: Jimbocho Near Tokyo Dome. Tokyo's used bookstore district has vinyl records, vintage manga, old maps. Perfect lazy morning before the game.

3/24
Tuesday
Shibuya
Shibuya + Shimokitazawa
🎵
Tower Records Shibuya 9 floors of music. Japanese pressings, city pop, jazz, experimental. Budgeting extra time here for sure.
🎮
Pokemon Center Shibuya Hit this early before crowds build up. Grab merch, plushies, limited Shibuya exclusives.
📸
Tokyo Photographic Art Museum (TOP)suggested In Ebisu, a 10-min walk from Shibuya. World-class photography exhibitions in a beautiful space. Usually 2-3 shows running at once.
🎸
Shimokitazawa — evening The real indie music neighborhood in Tokyo, 20 min from Shibuya. Dozens of tiny live venues, vinyl shops, vintage clothing, great cafes. Bookoff Plus in Meguro is still worth a stop for used vinyl during the day.
3/25
Wednesday
Ariake
Ossan Rental + Small Worlds 🌟
👴
Ossan Rental — morningreserve now Book at ossan.com — rent a middle-aged Japanese man for ¥1,000/hr to hang out, give advice, or wander with us. Wildly fun. Book a 2-3hr morning slot.
🔬
Small Worlds Tokyo — afternoonbook ahead In Ariake (Yurikamome line, Ariake-Tennis-no-Mori sta). Two full Evangelion areas: the NERV hangar with Unit-00/01/02 launching, and miniature Tokyo-III with the city sinking underground when Angels attack. Also Sailor Moon, Space Center, and a fantasy Global Village. The day/night cycle changes every 30 min. Pro move: pay for the Figure Residency and get a 1/80-scale 3D scan of yourself placed in the Eva exhibit for a year.
🥂
High-rise bar — evening Great way to cap the day with city views at night. Shibuya Sky (Scramble Square 46F) is a stunning rooftop deck worth doing at sunset. For drinks, the New York Bar at Park Hyatt Shinjuku (the Lost in Translation bar) is iconic, or Bar High Five in Ginza for serious cocktails.
3/26
Thursday
Ueno / Yanaka
Sakura + Yanaka + Asakusa
🌸
Ueno Park — sakura picnic Konbini haul in the morning, blanket in the park. Late March should be peak bloom. Getting there before 11am for a good spot.
🏘️
Yanaka — afternoon wander Old Tokyo neighborhood that survived the war. Yanaka Ginza shotengai for snacks: melon pan, curry bread, taiyaki. Feels like stepping back 60 years.
🎨
Yanaka Cemetery Sounds strange but genuinely beautiful. Tree-lined paths, old tombstones, very peaceful especially with sakura overhead.
☕
Kayaba Coffee Legendary old-school kissaten in Yanaka, open since 1938. Egg salad sandwich and coffee before heading to Asakusa.
🥢
Chopstick making workshop — Asakusabook ahead Asakusa is 15 min walk or one stop east of Yanaka. 60-90 min workshops where we shape and lacquer our own chopsticks. Search "chopstick making Tokyo" on Airbnb Experiences or Voyagin.
3/27
Friday
Shinjuku
Shinjuku Full Day + Jazz Night
🌿
Shinjuku Gyoen — morning One of Tokyo's best parks, beautiful in late March sakura season. No alcohol allowed (unlike Ueno) but very serene and worth it.
🏛️
Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building — afternoon Free observation deck on the 45th floor, one of the best views in Tokyo and completely free. Two towers, both worth going up. Gets great late afternoon light. 5 min walk from Shinjuku station west exit.
🍢
Omoide Yokocho — late afternoon into evening "Memory Lane": a narrow alley of tiny yakitori stalls, smoke-filled and nostalgic. Better in the late afternoon before the full evening rush. Grab skewers and a beer, then wander into Golden Gai after.
🌃
Golden Gai + Kabukicho wander A maze of 200+ tiny bars, each seats 6-8 people. Wander in, pick a vibe. Some have a ¥500 cover. Then walk through Kabukicho for the full neon-drenched chaos experience.
🎷
Jazz bar — late night Cap the last full night with live jazz. Top picks: Shinjuku Pit Inn (legendary basement venue since 1966, best musicians in Japan, shows ¥3,000-5,500), Jazz Cafe DUG (Haruki Murakami's old haunt, brick interior, very atmospheric), or Jazz Bar Samurai (cozy, hundreds of maneki neko cats, signed album covers everywhere).
